# Lean Training Portal Blueprint

## Problem to solve

The admin and client areas should not behave like a generic portal. They should support the full Lean training journey from cohort setup to live delivery, assessment, progress tracking, and certification readiness.

## What the old structure was not solving

### Client side

- Learners could not quickly see the next session, required prework, or what module was coming next.
- The resource experience behaved like a file list instead of a syllabus-linked learning library.
- Exams, practical assignments, feedback, and completion evidence were not central to the experience.
- Progress was implied by generic cards instead of showing attendance, capability growth, and readiness.

### Admin side

- The primary navigation focused on website and portal operations instead of training delivery.
- There was no first-class workspace for cohorts, session readiness, learner risk, or grading queues.
- Syllabus management, resource publishing, and assessment release were disconnected.
- Progress signals were not structured around learner completion, pass rates, attendance, and certification.

## Target client workflow

1. Know what is next.
2. Prepare for the upcoming session.
3. Follow the syllabus module by module.
4. Complete assessments and practical tasks.
5. Access the right materials for the right module.
6. Track progress, evidence, and readiness.
7. Ask for support without leaving the learning journey.

## Target admin workflow

1. Design the programme, cohort, roster, and pathway.
2. Prepare sessions, prework, facilitators, and materials.
3. Deliver live sessions and capture attendance/actions.
4. Release and mark assessments with feedback.
5. Track cohort health, learner risk, and completion.
6. Govern resources, syllabus versions, and certification.
